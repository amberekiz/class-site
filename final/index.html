Para sa aking lola,

Mama Pinang

↓

Growing up, I was not the closest to my great-grandmother. I would visit her every Sunday for 13 years straight, but always felt that there was this barrier between us. Maybe it was because of our age gap, but I always felt unsure on how to approach her. Most of our interactions for the many years I have known her for ranged from paying my respects to having a meal several seats apart.


A few months have gone by since she has passed away and it has given me the chance to reflect more on her. The more I think about her, the more I realize how much she has impacted my life and how important she is to my family. With this website, I want to cherish her memory and all that she has done for my mom, me, and so many others. I hope that Mama Pinang will continue to live in our hearts and that the mark she has made in this world will never be forgotten.

My great-grandmother's name is Pinang Rufina Saldaña. We all call her by Mama Pinang.

She was born in Vigan, Ilocos Sur in the Philippines on November 6, 1920. She was the seventh child among her nine siblings.

She was loving and kind, and enjoyed many things in life.

She loved to dance and party.

She loved to cook, especially for the family.

She enjoyed playing mahjong with friends.

She liked to collect jewelry.

She loved fashion and bright colors.

She liked to travel.

She loved the musical My Fair Lady starring Julie Andrews.
